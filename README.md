Sugarcane Leaf Disease Detection is an application of Machine Learning and Computer Vision used to automatically identify diseases in sugarcane leaves from images. The system analyzes leaf photos to detect common diseases such as red rot, leaf scald, and rust by recognizing patterns, color changes, and texture abnormalities.

Typically, models like Convolutional Neural Networks are trained on labeled datasets of healthy and diseased leaves. Once trained, the model can classify new images and help farmers or agricultural experts take timely action.

This technology improves crop monitoring, reduces manual inspection effort, and supports early disease detection, ultimately increasing yield and minimizing losses in agriculture.
